6. The Speaker's Body
  Posture, facial expression, gestures, eye contact--all affect the way listeners respond to a speaker.
  People trust their ears less than their eyes.

  PERSONAL APPEARANCE
  Every speakers is expected by her or his audience to exhibit a personal appearance in keeping with the occasion of the speech.
  Personal appearance plays an important role in speechmaking.

  MOVEMENT
  Quirks of movement usually stem from nervousness.
  As important as how you act during the speech is what you do just before you begin and after you finish.

  GESTURES
  Few aspects of delivery cause students more anguish than deciding what to do with their hands.
  The primary rule is this: Whatever gestures you make should not distract from your message.

7. Practicing Delivery
  You will do little to improve your speech delivery unless you practice the right things in the right ways.

  Five-step method.
  a. Go through your preparation outline aloud to check how what you have written translates into spoken discourse.
  b. Prepare your speaking outline.
  c. Practice the speech aloud several times using only the speaking outline.
  d. Practice the speech in front of a mirror to check for eye contact and distracting mannerisms.And then in front of friends, roommates, family members.
  e. Give your speech a dress rehearsal.

  A single practice session is rarely enough.
